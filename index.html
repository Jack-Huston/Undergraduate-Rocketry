Solid Rocket Motor – Build, Test & Failure

Assembly & Integration

Build and assembly of a composite wound R-class motor. Assisted with the functional testing of the motor casing and prepared hardware for the field static fire test.

Installing the R-class motor in its stand for proof-pressure testing

Test & Catastrophic Failure

The R-motor was static fired in a field to validate propellant and casing manufacturing methods. During the static fire test, the porous propellant fractured and chunks of propellant obstructed the throat which resulted in a rapid pressure rise. The aft end of the motor sheared as the pressure exceeded burst pressure; the casing launched upward; partially burnt casings remained at the stand.

  • Observed grain chunks exiting nozzle consistent with fracturing resulting in throat occlusion
  • Melted/failed stand components resulted in difficult failure analysis post-test
  • Root cause: propellant porosity → fracture → blockage → over-pressure
Failure sequence: nominal burn → casing fails → over-pressure separation → failure
Aftermath at the stand: melted hardware and aluminum oxide residue

Outcome

Following the explosion, work focused on firings of smaller solid motors to develop the propellant, grain structures, and nozzle development to prevent burn-through. This work continued following my time on the team, but culminated in a successful launch of the "Mamba" rocket flying on a Q-class motor, which broke the record for highest thrust SRM produced by a student team and reached an altitude of 89,000 ft in 2025.
