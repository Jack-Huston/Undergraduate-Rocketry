Solid Rocket Motor – Build, Test & Failure

Assembly & Integration

Helped with the build and install of the R-class motor on the proof-pressure stand and prepared hardware for the field static fire test.

I collaborated across mechanical and test subteams to coordinate stand fit checks, plumbing clearances, and instrumentation routing prior to rollout.

Installing the R-class motor in its stand for proof-pressure testing

Test & Catastrophic Failure

During the static fire test, porous propellant fractured, chunks of propellant obstructed the throat, which resulted in a rapid pressure rise. The aft end of the motor separated as the pressure exceeded burst pressure; the casing launched upward; partially burnt casings remained at the stand.

  • Observed grain chunks exiting nozzle consistent with fracturing resulting in throat occlusion
  • Melted/failed stand components resulted in difficult failure analysis post-test
  • Root cause: propellant porosity → fracture → blockage → over-pressure
Failure sequence: nominal burn → throat blockage → over-pressure separation → aftermath
Aftermath at the stand: melted hardware and aluminum oxide residue

Outcome

Following the explosion, work focused on firings of smaller solid motors to develope the propellant, grain structures, and nozzle development to prevent burn-through. This work continued following my time on the team, but culminated in a successful launch of the "Mamba" rocket flying on a Q-class motor, which broke the record for highest thrust SRM produced by a student team.
